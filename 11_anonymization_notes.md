# Anonymization Notes

## Purpose

This document explains the anonymization decisions adopted in this repository.

The repository was prepared for double-anonymous review and contains instructional artifacts related to a project-based Software Engineering education experience focused on sustainability-oriented requirements.

The goal is to support transparency and possible adaptation by other educators while avoiding identification of authors, students, stakeholders, projects, or institutions.

## General anonymization approach

The materials in this repository were reviewed and adapted to remove or generalize information that could identify the educational context, authors, institution, students, stakeholders, or specific projects.

The repository focuses on instructional artifacts rather than original student submissions.

## Removed or avoided information

The materials do not include:

- names of authors;
- names of students;
- names of instructors;
- names of stakeholders;
- names of clients or partner organizations;
- names of institutions;
- logos or visual identity elements;
- e-mail addresses;
- contact information;
- links to institutional pages;
- links to learning management systems;
- links to original online forms;
- links to project repositories;
- original student submissions;
- grades or individual feedback;
- identifiable project names;
- screenshots containing institutional information;
- documents containing personal data;
- documents containing sensitive contextual information.

## Adaptation of original forms

Some review and self-assessment instruments included in this repository were adapted from forms used during the course.

The following elements were removed or generalized:

- e-mail fields;
- student names;
- project names;
- client or stakeholder names;
- contact information;
- links to online forms;
- institutional references;
- timestamps;
- editing URLs;
- fields that could identify specific students, projects, stakeholders, or institutional settings.

The instruments are provided as reusable educational artifacts rather than as original data collection forms.

## Student-produced artifacts

Student-produced artifacts are not shared in their original form.

This decision was made because original student artifacts may contain contextual information related to:

- students;
- teams;
- stakeholders;
- clients;
- project names;
- specific health-related contexts;
- institutional processes;
- course logistics;
- feedback received during the course.

Even when names are removed, original artifacts may still contain indirect identifiers or contextual clues.

## Examples included in the repository

Examples included in this repository are anonymized, generalized, or synthetic.

They are intended to illustrate the type of reasoning expected in the educational activity, especially the transformation of sustainability concerns into non-functional requirements, acceptance criteria, and verification evidence.

The examples do not reproduce student artifacts in their original form.

## Repository naming and metadata

The repository name and description were chosen to be neutral and descriptive.

They do not include:

- author names;
- institutional names;
- course names that could identify the institution;
- research group names;
- project names;
- event-specific identifiers;
- links to author profiles or institutional pages.

## Use during double-anonymous review

During double-anonymous review, this repository should be accessed only through the anonymous link provided in the paper.

The original repository URL should not be cited in the submitted version of the paper.

## Public availability after acceptance

If the paper is accepted, the instructional materials may be curated and made publicly available in an identified online repository.

In that case, author, institutional, and citation information may be added to the public version.

Student-produced artifacts will only be shared publicly when all of the following conditions are met:

- they are properly anonymized;
- their use is authorized for academic dissemination;
- they do not contain sensitive or identifying contextual information;
- sharing them is ethically appropriate.

## Summary of anonymization decisions

| Material type | Included in this repository? | Anonymization decision |
|---|---|---|
| Activity descriptions | Yes | Generalized and anonymized. |
| Classroom templates | Yes | Generalized and anonymized. |

| Sustainability-oriented requirements template | Yes | Generalized and anonymized. |

| Grades or individual feedback | No | Not included due to privacy and identification risks. |

| Institutional logos or names | No | Removed to preserve anonymity. |
| Examples | Yes | Anonymized, generalized, or synthetic. |
