# Course Context

This document summarizes the educational context in which the experience was conducted.

## Course setting

The experience was conducted in an undergraduate Software Engineering I course, offered in the third semester of a health-related computing program.

The course introduces students to fundamental concepts and practices in Software Engineering, with emphasis on requirements, product planning, software modeling, teamwork, and iterative development.

## Course offerings

The experience was conducted in three course offerings:

- 2024/1
- 2025/1
- 2026/1

Across these offerings, the same general pedagogical structure was maintained, although the projects, stakeholder participation, and classroom discussions varied according to the specific problems addressed in each semester.

## Student participation

Each course offering involved approximately 30 students, organized into teams.

On average, each offering included approximately six projects, with one project developed by each team.

## Project domain

The projects focused on real digital health software problems.

The problems were related to health-related contexts and involved issues such as stakeholder needs, sensitive data, usability, accessibility, workflow support, information management, and continuity of care.

## Stakeholder involvement

Real stakeholders participated directly in two course offerings, especially during the presentation and discussion of results.

In one course offering, stakeholders provided the problems, but did not participate directly in the final discussion.

## Main activities

The experience involved the following sequence of activities:

1. Product vision definition and review
2. Product backlog construction and review
3. UML modeling
4. Sustainability-oriented requirements activity
5. Presentation and discussion of results

## Sustainability-oriented activity duration

The sustainability-oriented activity involved:

- approximately three hours of in-person classroom work;
- approximately four hours of extra-class work;
- approximately three additional hours of in-person presentation and discussion of results.

## Main assessed artifacts

The main assessed artifacts were:

- product vision;
- product backlog;
- UML component diagrams;
- UML activity diagrams;
- UML state diagrams.

Students received formative feedback during the development of the artifacts and additional feedback after the final presentation and discussion of results.
